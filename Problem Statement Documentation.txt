Gen-Z and career struggles using 5W1H Framework



INTRODUCTION:

Today, many Gen Z people are getting jobs and quitting within months. This is not just a personal problem, it's a pattern repeating across companies and industries worldwide.

Gen-Z are ambitious and tech-savvy, but often confused and unhappy at work. The question is: Is it because they expect too much or because workplaces haven't changed much?

This report uses 5W1H Framework to break down the problem from both sides - what Gen Z feels and what employers experience.



WHAT: Understanding the problem

What is the actual problem?
Understanding the gap between what Gen Z expect and what employers offer.
What kind of work environment makes Gen Z uncomfortable or unhappy?
Environments that are rigid, toxic or dismissive. They struggle in office where there is no work life balance, or where they are asked to do unpaid overtime.
What challenges do employers feel while managing Gen-Z?
Employers struggle with frequent job switching and high expectations.


WHY: Why does the problem exist?

Why do Gen-Z employees quit jobs quickly?
Because they often don't feel emotionally connected to the job. They prioritize their mental health and personal values over job security.
Why is there a gap between job expectation and reality?
Because social media often creates ideal expectations but in reality, most careers require time, experience and gradual growth.
Why do employers struggle to retain Gen-Z employees?
Many organizations still follow traditional workplace practices that focus on fixed schedules, strict hierarchies and slower career progression.


WHO: Who is affected by the issue?

Who experiences the biggest impact of this issue?
It affects both Gen-Z and employers as well as the Stakeholders involved like HR professionals, business partners, etc.
Who can help solve the problem?
It can be solved by the combined efforts of employers, HR Professionals, educational institutions, mentors and Gen-z themselves by better communication, realistic expectations, and mutual understanding.
Who benefits if the problem gets solved?
Everyone. Gen-z will be happy in their careers with improved mental health. Employers benefit due to reduced turnover ratio leading to stronger and more innovative teams.


WHEN: When does the problem occur?

When do Gen-Z employees begin feeling dissatisfied?
The problem often starts during the first few months of employment when expectations and reality begin to clash.
When do Gen-Z employees start actively looking for a new job?
Most Gen-Z start looking for new jobs when there is no salary hike, no growth opportunity, no feedback system, or a toxic work culture.
When did this problem become a major trend- was it always there?
The COVID-19 pandemic changed how people work, making Gen-Z expect more flexibility, better work-life balance, and healthier workplaces.


WHERE: Where does this problem happen?

Where does this mismatch show up most?
The problem is most visible in IT, banking, BPO/Customer service and larhe corporate companies.
Where do Gen-Z people express their concerns?
They heavily use LinkedIn, Reddit, X, and Instagram to dicuss job experiences and workplace horror stories.
Where can employers learn about employee expectations?
Employers can gather information through employee surveys, feedback meetings, exit interviews or performance discussions.


HOW: How to solve the problem?

How can employers better support Gen-Z employees?
They can support flexible work arrangements, provide regular feedback, create a positive work culture and offer career growth opportunities.
How can gen-Z employees improve workplace relationships?
They should set realistic expectations, communicate their concerns clearly, and be willing to learn and adapt.


CONCLUSION:

The report shows that the main issue is gap in expectations and communication. Gen-Z value flexibility, growth, and well-being while employers value commitment, performance and stability.



Better communication and mutual understanding can help improve job satisfaction, employee retention, and career success for both sides.
